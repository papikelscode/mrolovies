HOW TO ORDER ’ YOUR PRIORITIES


Priority is something we give precedence because of it’s urgency or importance. To prioritize means to arrange in order of importance. Priorities therefore are “Pre-decisions” we make to decide in advance what we’ll give ourselves to.

Priorities become a grid to help us distinguish opportunity from distraction. Priorities as filters helps us to sift daily decisions to make sure we keep on track.

Being completely enamored with the present state of things. Its deceptive because you assume that you are in control of your circumstances or that you have arrived at that state at your behest and could decide how things proceed.


When we make priorities in advance it helps us reduce the pressure we feel when we must make decisions under fire. Many mostly fail because they do not manage their priorities well. Without intending to, people will ask you to do things that meet their needs but do not necessarily match your priorities, especially if you have something to offer. So establish your purpose and let it define your priorities, then make decisions on the basis of your priorities not your pressures.


Since no man can do everything, necessitating the need for choices, priorities will help us determine how we spent our limited time and money. These points are guides that will help us set our priorities-

  1. Do not spend time on those that care about you the least. Rather pour yourself on those that will cry at your funeral, those that can’t live without you or you without them.
  2. Never do anything someone else can do. Concentrate on things you do very well and only you can do.
  3. Distinguish opportunity from distraction. Many a times distraction comes disguised as opportunities. Priority setting help us distinguish opportunity. Focus is the art of exclusion.
  4. Recognise the diffy from distraction.
  5. Differentiate between a good idea and a God idea. We are naturally inclined to act on the impulse of a good idea. But many at times a good idea gets in the way of a God idea.


Prayer removes the impulse of a good idea born out of human ingenuity, but not of God. To habitually distinguish between a good idea and a God idea, PRAY then PLAN. PRIORITY AREARS: Relationship with God, wife, children, finance, health (including leisure and rest). Also set priority for your work and or ministry.
