PERSPECTIVE VS PRESUMPTION


Col. 1:9-12 Says may you be filled with the knowledge of His will with all wisdom and spiritual understanding. Simply put it means being filled with the knowledge of God’s will with the accurate godly perspective.

Perspective is understanding something because you are seeing it from a larger frame of reference. It is the ability to perceive how things interrelate and then judge their comparative importance. In a spiritual sense, it means seeing life from God’s point of view. In the Bible, the words understanding, wisdom and discernment all have to do with perspective, the opposite of perspective is Presumption.

We ask how I can willingly become vulnerable again after one has been so abused, exploited or even stolen from. It is often too difficult a prospect to consider.


Ps. 103:7 says God made known His ways to Moses and His deeds to the people of Israel. The people of Israel got to see what God did, but Moses got to understand why God did it. This is the difference between knowledge and perspective. Knowledge is learning what God has said and done while perspective is understanding why God said it or did it. It answers the “witty” questions of life. Perspective not mere knowledge is what provides stability in people’s lives.


However when we are presumptuous it prevents us from seeing things from God’s perspective thus making us unable to fully please Him. Seeing things from God’s perspective is what makes us mature Christians and also a dependable axe. Always remember that godly perspective proceeds from the heart rather than from the mind. When our heart is appropriately pruned it supplies the mind with the accurate interpretation of information which the mind will work on.

Now when we see things from God’s perspective we shall:

  1. Be fruitful in every good work.
  2. Constantly increase in the knowledge of God.
  3. We receive strength to enable us to be patient while doing battle.
  4. We always offer thanksgiving to God as we experience the promises He made to the saints.
